From Godfall to War: A Scholar’s Account of the Shardborn Age

By Thalius Mern, Historian of the Unified Academy of Omens and Records — Circa Year 50 Post-Godfall

Prologue: The Godfall (Year 0)

Following the events that concluded the last recorded era, the known pantheon of gods was shattered, rewritten, or reborn. Mortal scholars now call this the Godfall—an age in which divine essences reemerged across the Material Plane, not as restored deities, but as new beings, amalgamating ancient domains and foreign powers.

These gods did not descend in unison, nor in peace. Each "broke through" into the world at different points, often accompanied by catastrophic miracles or revelations. (See the Godfall Event Timeline, Appendix I.) Most nations treated this new age with awe and trepidation. But it would not be long before reverence turned into ambition.

The Road to War: Timeline of Events

Year 12 PG (Post-Godfall)

Valiki Laz manifests during the Festival of Two Veils in Demori. Her split nature—light and night, creation and destruction—becomes a symbol of artistic and religious duality. Her cult becomes state-sponsored, particularly by cultural elites in Deviates and Demori.

Nalkira, the Drowned Flame, is seen erupting beneath the Bay of Smoke. Naval myths begin to swirl around her influence on tides and volcanic activity. Wildrune and Skyvard sailors begin ritual seawater burning rites, seen as barbaric by Demori theocrats.

Year 20 PG

Orunax’s scholars in Demori publish the “Thesis of Manifest Structure,” arguing that gods require mortal civilization to be stable. This becomes the foundation for Demori’s new Arcane-Divine Integration Doctrine. Meanwhile, Ikaros’ teachings begin to circulate in Skyvard’s floating citadels, advocating survival through adaptation rather than control.

Year 38 PG

Ylluvien the Dreaming Root begins appearing in shared visions among Wildrune shamans and Demorian astral scholars alike. Reports emerge of radiant flora blooming unnaturally deep in the Wildrune jungle.

Year 50 PG

Wildrune becomes officially known to the greater world. Its sudden appearance shifts allegiances and national interests from all surrounding nations.

Year 51 PG

Ylluvien’s Bloom is officially discovered by a Deviate expedition—an immense, shimmering tree pulsing with astral song and verdant light. The Twin Nations (Demori & Deviates) declare it a universal relic, claiming religious and scholarly dominion.

Wildrune druids reject these claims, declaring a Pact of Thorns with local spirits and ancestral memories. Hostilities begin with small skirmishes, burning of outposts, and the destruction of arcane monoliths. Meanwhile, magical storms begin forming in Skyvard, worsening by the month. Floating cities destabilize. Sky-farming collapses. Panic spreads.

Year 55 PG

The Sky Shattering occurs: a celestial rupture tears across Skyvard’s upper strata, dropping entire isles into the sea. Survivors cite thunder in the shape of a bird and lightning speaking with human tongues.

In response, the storm-god Ikaros performs a divine rite, sacrificing his voice in the highest mountain peak. From the storm emerges the Aethergale Vanguard, a cadre of aerial knights imbued with storm essence. Skyvard’s High Council blames Demori’s continued magical tampering—particularly their use of divine frequencies to harvest energy from Ylluvien’s Bloom—as the cause of the storm cascade.

Year 57 PG

The First Flames of War erupt. Demori and Deviates establish the Sanctified Bloom Province in southern Wildrune.

  • Wildrune tribes ambush a diplomatic caravan, reportedly killing four Deviate scholars and scattering divine relics into a living memory loop.
  • Skyvard joins Wildrune, citing divine right and shared persecution. The Aethergale Vanguard strikes the Demorian border port of Vireth-Kaan.
  • Demori and Deviates respond with arcane conscription and the activation of their first Living War Monoliths, semi-sentient divine machines powered by relics and oaths.

Year 58 PG – Present

The war escalates into full-scale conflict across multiple fronts. Theological debate has given way to militant crusades and wild, divine weather anomalies.

Three primary fronts dominate:

  1. The Bloomfront (Wildrune) – Jungle warfare, memory magic, druidic insurgency vs fortified arcane redoubts.
  2. The Skystorm Front (Skyvard's Perimeter) – Aethergale Vanguard storm raids against floating mageforts and temple-cannons.
  3. The Sea of Masks – Naval warfare intermixed with divine illusions from followers of Fyrrax.

The Fractured Pantheon: The New Divines

“The old gods are dust. The new gods wear their bones like crowns.”

Fifty years ago, something catastrophic shattered the heavens. When the Divine Veil was broken, the essences of the old gods bled into the world—colliding, merging, reforming. Mortals, beasts, and even ideas served as vessels for reincarnation. The result is a pantheon of dual-aspect, contradictory gods—new deities who embody tension instead of purity.

Pantheon Overview
Each god below is a paradox made divine. Their worshippers often split into sects, each leaning into one aspect while wrestling with the other. The divine magic granted by these gods is often unpredictable—but potent.

Ikaros, The Whale Above

Domains: Sky • Patience • Tyranny

A leviathan that drifts across the heavens. Its great eye never blinks. Represents the vast openness of freedom and the crushing weight of order. Seen by some as a gentle shepherd of wind, by others as a god-emperor in the clouds.

Symbol: A whale chained to a cloud.

Clerics: Often meteorologists, sky captains, or judges.

Tirixa, The Bird Beneath

Domains: Sea • Secrets • Rebirth

A luminous avian being submerged in the deepest ocean, flapping slowly in the dark. Tales say every one of her feathers is a sunken truth. Her cults teach salvation through drowning, and her waters cleanse memory.

Symbol: A feathered wave cresting over a black sun.

Clerics: Spies, undertakers, and therapists of the soul.

Vorel & Kavra, The Split Flame

Domains: Love • War

A fused god of passion—two eternal lovers burned together in fire. Vorel is the flame that nourishes; Kavra is the flame that consumes. Their clergy argue endlessly about whether love is union or destruction.

Symbol: A heart made of interlocked swords.

Clerics: Matchmakers, soldiers, and vengeance-seekers alike.

Orunax, The Bleeding Quill

Domains: Knowledge • Madness

Once a scribe, now a writhing tangle of parchment wings and eye-covered ink glands. He records everything. Every thought, dream, and falsehood. His followers believe knowledge is holy, even if it corrodes the mind.

Symbol: An open book with a screaming mouth.

Clerics: Scholars, heretics, and mad poets.

Ylluvien, The Blooming Wound

Domains: Nature • Death

A rotting tree goddess, whose bark is graveflesh and whose flowers only bloom in cemeteries. She teaches that all things must die to live again. Worshipped by druids, necromancers, and gravekeepers alike.

Symbol: A skull sprouting petals from its mouth.

Clerics: Floramancers, plague doctors, caretakers of ruin.

Fyrrax, The Mirrorforge

Domains: Creation • Lies

A sentient machine-god formed from discarded divine relics. His forge does not build what is, but what people believe to be true. Some say he’s the god of illusion, others of invention.

Symbol: A mask with a glowing anvil for a mouth.

Clerics: Artificers, cult leaders, illusionists.

Zethra, The Clockbound Heart

Domains: Time • Emotion

A massive heart impaled on a golden sundial, beating in erratic rhythms. Time is not linear to her—she responds to feelings, not chronology. Oracles claim she grants visions through grief, nostalgia, or heartbreak.

Symbol: A cracked hourglass dripping blood.

Clerics: Chronomancers, empaths, memory-keepers.

Molgrim, The Crownless

Domains: Justice • Chaos

An ancient child clad in molten armor, dragging the severed crown of a tyrant. Believes all systems must fall for justice to be born anew. Both law-bringers and revolutionaries claim him as their patron.

Symbol: A scale set on fire.

Clerics: Executioners, protestors, vigilantes.

Valiki Laz, The Twice-Faced Star

Domains: Light • Night

A theatrical mask splits the day from the night. She often appears in carnival attire. Night and day are two sides of the same coin for her devotees.

Symbol: A theatrical mask split vertically—sun on one side, crescent moon on the other.

Clerics: Festival lords, assassins with code, war-bards, vigilantes, playwrights.

Ezzir, The Hollow Muse

Domains: Inspiration • Emptiness

A shimmering, genderless artist with a face that reflects the viewer’s most painful longing. Some offer silence instead of song, crafting sculptures and then shattering them to honor the Muse’s truth.

Symbol: A paintbrush drawing a void.

Clerics: Artists, nihilists, dream-binders.

Mavros, The Sorrow-King

Domains: Joy • Grief

His temples are half festivals, half funerary rites—blaring music played beside silent meditation. Priests wear robes of black and gold, laughing and weeping in rhythm.

Symbol: A laughing skull with a tear on one cheek.

Clerics: Grief counselors, party planners, bards of elegy and ecstasy.

Tzel, The Evercoil

Domains: Change • Memory

A serpent made of moments—scales glinting with visions of the past and possible futures. Worship of Tzel is the worship of personal evolution and the rejection of ancestral chains.

Symbol: A spiral serpent devouring its own shadow.

Clerics: Amnesiacs, reincarnationists, shapeshifting druids.

Ignavar, The Ember King

Domains: Hope • Despair

A hooded king who carries a single candle in endless night. Its flame never goes out, no matter how much he wishes it would. His temples are pitch black but for one flame.

Symbol: A candle with melting eyes.

Clerics: War priests, hospice clerics, lightbearers in enemy camps.

Avenyx, The Threadkeeper

Domains: Destiny • Freedom

A spider-like being with a loom for a thorax and endless threads spun from the souls of mortals. Cults spin literal webs of string across temples. Rituals involve cutting and tying strands to represent altered destinies.

Symbol: A web with one golden thread torn loose.

Clerics: Oracles, rebels, fate-defiers.

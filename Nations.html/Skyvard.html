I. OVERVIEW

Skyvard was once a unified island kingdom of floating cities, famed for its skyborne architecture and a culture that danced upon clouds. But fifty years ago, the Godfall ruptured the skies. Unstable arcane weather patterns—lightning that never ceases, flying beasts warped by storm magic, and island-breaking cyclones—ravaged the realm. Entire cities fell from the heavens, and Skyvard fractured into a loose network of survivor bastions strung together by storm-riders, airships, and desperate faith. They now call themselves the Skyvard Conclave: a people forged in loss, shaped by elemental chaos, and blaming Demori and Deviates for breaking the world.

II. POLITICAL STRUCTURE

Skyvard no longer has a central monarchy. Instead, it is ruled by a Storm Conclave, made up of five High Wardens who represent the last remaining major skyholds. Each leads one of the following holdings:

  • Thal'Korran – Fortress of storms and home of the Aethergale Vanguard.
  • Varixspire – Aether-powered port city and engineering haven.
  • Kaelmar Cradle – Agricultural oasis sustained by druidic stormbinding.
  • Torrinthal Bastion – Skyvard’s remaining arcane library, struck twice by skyfire.
  • The Maw – A lawless trading post ruled by mercenary votes and elemental treaties.

The Conclave meets irregularly via projected crystal simulacra—a necessity in a land where travel itself is perilous.

III. RELIGION & PROPHECY

Ikaros, the reborn God of Sky and Sovereignty, is the state’s central deity. It is said that he fused his will into the first storm that saved Skyvard’s capital from ruin. His prophecy—"We will rise as those below break"—is etched into banners, blade edges, and the hearts of Skyvardian youth. Other deities have shrines, but worship is pragmatic: gods who can protect from skyfire earn faith.

IV. MILITARY & THE AETHERGALE VANGUARD

Skyvard’s martial force is led by the Aethergale Vanguard, a knightly order empowered by divine rites performed atop storm-spires. Each knight is chosen by Ikaros himself—or so they claim—and carries lightning-forged arms, enchanted cloaks, and unique warbrands called Tempest Sigils.

The Vanguard operates in flights of 3–5 members, with elite “Cloudcallers” commanding localized weather phenomena. Their windwalk rituals let them launch rapid strikes and disappear like a breeze.

Skyvard also deploys:

  • Arc-Gliders: Floating siege platforms mounted with ballistae and storm-warped spellgems.
  • Windjammers: Sleek airships capable of entering the heart of a maelstrom.

V. CULTURE & ATTITUDE

Skyvardians are blunt, resourceful, and unyielding. They prize personal adaptability, lightning-fast decision-making, and loyalty to one's stormhold above all else. Children learn to fly before they walk. Falling is considered an initiation. A stormborn who refuses the winds is no true citizen. Many citizens undergo rites to bond with a storm—earning small arcane adaptations, such as crackling veins, storm-sight, or atmospheric resistance.

VI. HOSTILITIES WITH DEMORI & DEVIATES

Skyvard holds Demori and Deviates responsible for disturbing the arcane balance that led to the atmospheric collapse—especially after rumors of divine experiments gone awry reached their storm-halls. The colonization of Wildrune is seen as confirmation: the lowlanders break what they do not understand and now seek to tame even the Wildheart, a crime Skyvard considers an existential threat. Skyvard joined Wildrune in declaring war, seeing themselves as the last defenders of the world’s elemental soul.

VII. RECENT EVENTS

  • The Maw was overtaken by a storm-forged warlord claiming to wield a divine thunder artifact. Some suspect they’re a rogue Aethergale experiment.
  • Stormbinders in Kaelmar have begun growing crops that speak or hum during rainfall. The crops are sentient, and no one knows why.
  • Ikaros appeared in a prophetic dream to a dozen children across Skyvard, warning them of “the Forge Rooted in Death”—interpreted to mean Demori’s Wildheart experiments.
